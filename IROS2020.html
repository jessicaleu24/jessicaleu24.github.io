Safe and Coordinated Hierarchical Receding Horizon Control for Mobile Manipulators


Jessica Leu, Liting Sun, and Masayoshi Tomizuka

Welcome! This website supplements our IROS 2020 submission, in which we presents presented a contact, estimation and planning framework that allows light-weighted mobile manipulators to actively collect information through physical contact and generate more feasible trajectory plans by explicitly integrating such information into motion planners so that the robot can handle dynamic changes and possible failures caused by environment uncertainties.

Mobile manipulators, benefiting from both mobility from the platform and agility from the arm, have many applications that involve physical contact with the environment such as picking up objects, placing objects, and opening doors. The uncertainties involved in these operations, however, can influence the robot dynamics and correspondingly its optimal operations. For instance, pushing doors with different stiffness might require the mobile manipulator to adjust itself to different poses. Without actively explore and estimate such uncertainties, traditional motion planning algorithms might generate a dynamically infeasible trajectory and lead to failure. To tackle this problem, we proposed a motion planning framework that handles contact-related uncertainties via active contacting, estimating, and planning (CEP). The CEP framework contains a probabilistic formulation to capture the characteristics of the contact-related uncertainties, a Bayesian update step to estimate the parameters that describe the uncertainties, and an optimization-based motion planner that integrates the estimated uncertainties. The framework is implemented on a TurtleBot3 with the OpenMANIPULATOR (TB3O) platform. We verify the performance through both simulations and experiments. The results show that the framework helps the robot plan more successfully in the presence of contact-related uncertainties.
